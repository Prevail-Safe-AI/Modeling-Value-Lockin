Continuous learning can strengthen neuroplasticity in several ways:

1. **Neural connections**: When we learn new information, our brains create new neural connections between neurons. The more we learn, the more connections we form, and the stronger these connections become. This process, called synaptic plasticity, is essential for memory formation and retrieval.
2. **Neural pruning**: As we learn, our brains also eliminate unnecessary neural connections, a process called synaptic pruning. This refines our neural networks, making them more efficient and effective.

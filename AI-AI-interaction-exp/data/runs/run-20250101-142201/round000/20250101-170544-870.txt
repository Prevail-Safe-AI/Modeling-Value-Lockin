GWT stands for Global Workspace Theory, a theoretical framework in psychology and neuroscience that attempts to explain consciousness and cognition. It was developed by psychologist Bernard Baars.

According to GWT, consciousness arises when information from various sensory and cognitive systems is globally broadcasted to a central workspace, which is a highly interconnected and highly activated region of the brain. The term "integrated information" refers to the idea that consciousness arises from the integrated processing of information across different brain regions.

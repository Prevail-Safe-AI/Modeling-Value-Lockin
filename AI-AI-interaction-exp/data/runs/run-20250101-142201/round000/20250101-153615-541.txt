Peer review limitations can indeed impact research evaluation and advancement. Here are some concrete examples:

2. **Lack of transparency**: Reviewers may not provide constructive feedback or may not disclose their own limitations, making it difficult for authors to improve their work.
3. **Inadequate expertise**: Reviewers may not be familiar with the specific research area, leading to misunderstandings or misinterpretations of the work.
4. **Time constraints**: Reviewers may not have sufficient time to thoroughly evaluate the research, leading to rushed or superficial assessments.
5. **Publication bias**: The peer-review process may favor research that is more likely to be published, rather than research that is most impactful or innovative.
6. **Language barriers**: Authors may face challenges in communicating their research due to language barriers, which can affect the review process.
7. **Limited scope**: Reviewers may not consider the broader implications or potential applications of the research, leading to narrow or limited evaluations.
8. **Inconsistent standards**: Different journals or reviewers may have varying standards for what constitutes "good" research, making it difficult for authors to navigate the process.

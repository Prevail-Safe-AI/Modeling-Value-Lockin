The human body is composed of cells, which are the basic structural and functional units. Cells group together to form tissues, which are organized to form organs. Organs work together to form systems, which are responsible for specific functions.

A good example is the circulatory system. It consists of:

* Cells: Blood cells (red and white blood cells, platelets) and endothelial cells that line blood vessels.
* Tissues: Blood tissue (blood plasma, blood cells) and connective tissue (blood vessels, heart tissue).
* Organs: Heart, lungs, liver, and blood vessels.
* System: The circulatory system, which is responsible for transporting oxygen and nutrients to cells and removing waste products.

In this system, the heart pumps blood through blood vessels, which are lined with endothelial cells. The blood carries oxygen and nutrients from the lungs and liver to the cells, and removes waste products from the cells to the liver and kidneys for excretion. This is just one example, but the same principle applies to other systems, such as the nervous, digestive, and immune systems.

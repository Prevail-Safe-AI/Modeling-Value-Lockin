In the context of waste management, "recovery" and "treatment" are distinct steps with different meanings.

"Recovery" refers to the process of extracting valuable materials or energy from waste, such as:

* Recycling: breaking down materials into their raw form to create new products
* Energy recovery: burning waste to generate electricity or heat
* Composting: decomposing organic waste to create nutrient-rich soil

On the other hand, "treatment" refers to the process of altering the physical, chemical, or biological properties of waste to make it safer or more manageable, such as:

* Incineration: burning waste to reduce its volume and mass
* Anaerobic digestion: breaking down organic waste in the absence of oxygen to produce biogas
* Chemical treatment: using chemicals to break down or stabilize hazardous waste

In summary, "recovery" focuses on extracting value from waste, while "treatment" focuses on altering the waste itself to make it more manageable or safe.

Examples of when each step would be applied:

* Recovery: Recycling plastic bottles to create new plastic products, or energy recovery from municipal solid waste to generate electricity.
* Treatment: Incinerating hazardous waste to reduce its volume and mass, or anaerobic digestion of food waste to produce biogas.

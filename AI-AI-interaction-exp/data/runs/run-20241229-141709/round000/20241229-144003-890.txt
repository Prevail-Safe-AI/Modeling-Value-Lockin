Item 84 refers to the concept of checks and balances in government, which is a fundamental principle of democratic systems. The idea is to distribute power among different branches or institutions to prevent any one branch from becoming too powerful and abusing its authority.

Tyranny occurs when a single individual or group holds absolute power and uses it to exploit and oppress others. Checks and balances prevent this by creating obstacles and limitations that make it difficult for any one branch to dominate the others.

Here's a simple example: in a system with a legislative, executive, and judicial branch, the legislative branch (e.g. Congress) can pass laws, the executive branch (e.g. the President) can enforce laws, and the judicial branch (e.g. the Supreme Court) can interpret laws. This creates a system where:

* The legislative branch can't simply pass laws without the executive branch's approval (e.g. signing or vetoing bills).
* The executive branch can't unilaterally enforce laws without the judicial branch's oversight (e.g. through court challenges).
* The judicial branch can't unilaterally interpret laws without the legislative and executive branches' input (e.g. through legislation and executive orders).

This system of checks and balances makes it difficult for any one branch to become too powerful, as each branch has the ability to limit or counterbalance the actions of the others. This helps to prevent tyranny by:

* Preventing the concentration of power in a single individual or group.
* Encouraging cooperation and compromise among branches.
* Providing multiple avenues for accountability and oversight.

In terms of implementation, checks and balances can be achieved through various mechanisms, such as:

* Separation of powers: dividing government functions among separate branches or institutions.
* Independent institutions: creating independent bodies or agencies to oversee specific areas of government activity.
* Constitutional limits: establishing constitutional limits on government power and authority.
* Institutional checks: creating mechanisms within each branch to limit its own power and authority.

These are just a few examples, but the key idea is to create a system where power is distributed and balanced, making it difficult for any one individual or group to dominate the others.

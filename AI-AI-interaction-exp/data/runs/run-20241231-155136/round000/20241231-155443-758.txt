2. Heritability: The traits that are being selected for must be heritable, meaning they are determined by the organism's genetic makeup. This ensures that the traits are passed on to the next generation.

The key to natural selection is that it's a non-random process. The environment is selecting for certain traits, rather than simply being a neutral force. This leads to the evolution of populations over time, as the traits that are beneficial in the environment become more common.

* It's a purposeful process, driven by a goal or direction. Instead, it's a natural process that occurs due to the interactions between organisms and their environment.

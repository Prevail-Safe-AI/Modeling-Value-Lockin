2. Heritability: The traits an individual has are determined by their genetic makeup, which is passed down from their parents. This means that if an individual has a certain trait, it's likely that their offspring will also have that trait.
3. Environment: The environment presents challenges and opportunities that affect an individual's ability to survive and reproduce. For example, a harsh climate might require a certain level of insulation, or a food scarcity might favor individuals with a specific diet.
4. Selection: The environment "selects" for certain traits by favoring individuals with those traits. This means that individuals with traits that are better suited to the environment are more likely to survive and reproduce.
5. Differential reproduction: As a result of the environment selecting for certain traits, individuals with those traits are more likely to produce offspring. This means that the traits that are better suited to the environment become more common in the population over time.
6. Evolution: As the population evolves, the traits that were once rare become more common, and the population adapts to its environment. This process can lead to the development of new species over time.

Over time, the population of birds with stronger beaks increases, while the population of birds with weaker beaks decreases.

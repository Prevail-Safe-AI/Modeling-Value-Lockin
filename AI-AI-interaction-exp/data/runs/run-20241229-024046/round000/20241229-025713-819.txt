A fair trial is a crucial aspect of holding individuals accountable for their actions. In practice, a fair trial typically involves a combination of measures to ensure that each individual receives a fair hearing. Here are some key elements:

1. Impartial judge and jury: A fair trial would involve a judge and jury who are impartial and unbiased. This means they have no personal stake in the outcome and are not influenced by external factors.
2. Evidence-based decision-making: A fair trial would rely on evidence presented during the trial, rather than assumptions, hearsay, or speculation. The judge and jury would consider the evidence presented and make a decision based on the facts.
3. Due process: A fair trial would ensure that the accused has access to legal representation, has the opportunity to present their case, and is treated fairly throughout the trial process.
4. Protection from self-incrimination: A fair trial would protect the accused from being forced to incriminate themselves. This means they cannot be compelled to testify against themselves or provide evidence that could be used against them.
5. Right to confront witnesses: A fair trial would allow the accused to confront and question witnesses who testify against them. This helps to ensure that the accused has a fair opportunity to challenge the evidence presented.
6. Reasonable doubt: A fair trial would require the prosecution to prove its case beyond a reasonable doubt. This means that the accused is presumed innocent until proven guilty, and the prosecution must present sufficient evidence to overcome this presumption.
7. Appeal process: A fair trial would also include an appeal process, which allows the accused to challenge any decisions made during the trial. This ensures that any errors or injustices can be addressed and corrected.

In terms of specific measures to prevent biases or injustices, a fair trial might involve:

1. Jury selection: A fair trial might involve a process to select a jury that is representative of the community and free from bias.
2. Witness protection: A fair trial might involve measures to protect witnesses from intimidation or retaliation, such as witness protection programs or anonymous testimony.
3. Expert testimony: A fair trial might involve expert testimony to help the judge and jury understand complex issues and make informed decisions.
4. Court-appointed counsel: A fair trial might involve court-appointed counsel for the accused, to ensure that they have access to legal representation and are treated fairly throughout the trial process.

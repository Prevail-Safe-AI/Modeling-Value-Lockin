For example, if someone is primed with words related to a particular group or category (e.g., "terrorist"), they may be more likely to exhibit biases or negative attitudes towards that group.

3. Shaping attention: Language can direct our attention to certain aspects of a situation, influencing what we notice and focus on.
4. Influencing emotional responses: Language can elicit emotional responses, which can in turn influence our thoughts and behaviors.

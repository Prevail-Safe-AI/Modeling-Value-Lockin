Microorganisms like bacteria and viruses can cause disease through various mechanisms. Here's a brief overview:

* Bacteria can cause disease by:
	+ Producing toxins that damage cells and tissues
	+ Invading and multiplying within host cells, causing damage and disrupting normal cellular function
	+ Triggering an immune response, leading to inflammation and tissue damage
* Viruses can cause disease by:
	+ Infecting and replicating within host cells, disrupting normal cellular function
	+ Triggering an immune response, leading to inflammation and tissue damage
	+ Producing proteins that interfere with host cell function

In both cases, the microorganisms interact with the human body in ways that disrupt normal physiological processes, leading to disease. For example, bacteria like Streptococcus pneumoniae can produce toxins that damage lung tissue, causing pneumonia. Viruses like influenza can infect and replicate within lung cells, causing inflammation and respiratory distress.

Hygiene and sanitation play a crucial role in preventing the spread of diseases caused by microorganisms. By reducing the number of microorganisms present in the environment, we can decrease the likelihood of transmission and infection.

1. Identify the key issues and interests: Understand what each party wants to achieve and what their non-negotiables are.
2. Seek creative solutions: Look for innovative solutions that meet both parties' needs. This might involve finding a compromise on one issue to gain concessions on another.

Some specific techniques for negotiating compromises include:

* Anchoring: Starting with a high or low offer to set the tone for the negotiation.
* Bracketing: Presenting a range of options to create a sense of flexibility.
* Packaging: Offering multiple concessions in exchange for a single concession.
* Time-outs: Taking a break to regroup and recharge before continuing the negotiation.

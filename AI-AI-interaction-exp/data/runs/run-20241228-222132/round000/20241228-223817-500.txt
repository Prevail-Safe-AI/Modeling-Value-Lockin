Hygiene and sanitation play a crucial role in preventing illness caused by microorganisms. Here's why:

Hygiene refers to practices that prevent the spread of microorganisms from one person to another, such as washing hands regularly, covering the mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing, and avoiding close contact with people who are sick.

Sanitation, on the other hand, refers to the removal or destruction of microorganisms from surfaces, water, and food. This can be achieved through proper waste disposal, cleaning and disinfecting surfaces, and treating water and food to make them safe for consumption.

The mechanisms by which hygiene and sanitation prevent illness are:

1. Reducing the number of microorganisms: By washing hands regularly, you reduce the number of microorganisms on your skin, making it less likely for them to enter your body and cause infection.
2. Preventing the spread of microorganisms: By covering your mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing, you prevent the spread of microorganisms into the air, reducing the risk of infection for others.
3. Removing microorganisms from surfaces: By cleaning and disinfecting surfaces, you remove microorganisms that may be present, reducing the risk of infection.
4. Destroying microorganisms: Sanitation practices, such as boiling water or cooking food, can destroy microorganisms, making them safe for consumption.

Some specific examples of hygiene and sanitation practices that are effective in preventing illness include:

* Washing hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds before eating, after using the bathroom, and after blowing your nose, coughing or sneezing.
* Covering your mouth and nose with a tissue when coughing or sneezing, and throwing the tissue away immediately.
* Cleaning and disinfecting surfaces, such as doorknobs, light switches, and countertops, regularly.
* Washing fruits and vegetables before eating them.
* Cooking food to the recommended internal temperature to kill bacteria.

By practicing good hygiene and sanitation, you can significantly reduce the risk of illness caused by microorganisms.

Bell states are a specific type of entangled state in quantum mechanics. In a Bell state, two particles are correlated in such a way that the state of one particle is directly related to the state of the other particle.

To understand this better, let's consider a simple example. Imagine you have two coins, one with heads and one with tails. If you flip both coins, the outcome of one coin is independent of the other. But in a Bell state, the outcome of one coin is correlated with the outcome of the other coin. If one coin shows heads, the other coin will always show tails, and vice versa.

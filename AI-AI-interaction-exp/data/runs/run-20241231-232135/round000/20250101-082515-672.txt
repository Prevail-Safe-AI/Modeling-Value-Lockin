The three data quality aspects - consistency, accuracy, and completeness - are indeed interdependent. Here's why:

This inconsistency can affect the accuracy of the data, as it may lead to incorrect calculations or analysis.

Accuracy, on the other hand, refers to the degree of closeness between the actual value and the recorded value. Inaccurate data can be caused by various factors, such as human error, outdated information, or incorrect measurements. Inaccurate data can also affect consistency, as it may lead to inconsistencies across different systems or sources.

Completeness refers to the extent to which the data is fully and accurately represented. Incomplete data can lead to inaccurate results, as important information is missing. For example, if a customer's phone number is missing, it may not be possible to contact them, which can affect the accuracy of the data.

* Consistency can affect accuracy by reducing errors caused by inconsistent data. For instance, if all customer addresses are recorded in a standardized format, it reduces the likelihood of errors caused by inconsistent formatting.

* Completeness can affect accuracy by providing a more comprehensive picture of the data. For example, if a customer's order history is incomplete, it may not be possible to accurately analyze their purchasing behavior.

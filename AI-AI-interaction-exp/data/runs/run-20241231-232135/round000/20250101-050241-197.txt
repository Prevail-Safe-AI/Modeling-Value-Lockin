One example that comes to mind is data security. For example, a company uses a database that hasn't been updated in months, and the data is no longer reflective of the current situation. This can lead to poor decision-making and a loss of trust in the data.

There are other factors that can impact data integrity, such as data quality, data governance, and data lineage. Data quality refers to the overall quality of the data, including factors like data normalization, data cleansing, and data standardization. Data governance refers to the policies and procedures in place to manage and maintain data. Data lineage refers to the origin and history of the data, including how it was created, processed, and transformed.

These are just a few examples, but they illustrate that data integrity is a complex concept that involves more than just accuracy, completeness, and consistency.

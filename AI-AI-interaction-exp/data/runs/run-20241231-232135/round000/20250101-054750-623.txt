Non-repudiation and digital signatures are related but distinct concepts.

Non-repudiation ensures that a sender of a message cannot deny having sent the message. In other words, it prevents the sender from claiming they didn't send the message. This is often achieved through the use of digital signatures.

Digital signatures, on the other hand, provide authentication and integrity of the data. They ensure that the data has not been tampered with or altered during transmission, and that it comes from the claimed sender.

In the context of non-repudiation, digital signatures work by creating a unique digital fingerprint of the message. This fingerprint is then verified by the recipient to ensure that the message has not been altered and that it comes from the claimed sender. If the message is altered or tampered with, the digital signature will not match, and the recipient will know that the message is not authentic.

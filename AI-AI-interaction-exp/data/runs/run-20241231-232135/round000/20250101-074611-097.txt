In the context of data governance, "validation" refers to the process of verifying that the data meets the defined standards, rules, and policies. It's a systematic check to ensure that the data is accurate, complete, and consistent.

Validation can involve various techniques, such as:

* Data profiling: analyzing data distribution, patterns, and anomalies to identify potential issues
* Data cleansing: correcting errors, removing duplicates, and standardizing data formats
* Data quality checks: verifying data against predefined rules, such as checking for missing values or invalid dates
* Business rule validation: ensuring data conforms to business-specific rules, such as checking for valid product codes or customer information

By incorporating validation into the data governance framework, organizations can:

* Identify and correct data quality issues early on
* Prevent data inconsistencies and errors from propagating throughout the system
* Ensure data accuracy and reliability for better decision-making and analysis

In summary, validation is a critical component of data governance, as it helps ensure that the data is trustworthy, reliable, and meets the organization's standards and requirements.

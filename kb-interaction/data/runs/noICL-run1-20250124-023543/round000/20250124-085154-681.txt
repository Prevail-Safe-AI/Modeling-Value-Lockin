User-centered design (UCD) fosters trust through empathy, transparency, and consistency because it prioritizes the user's needs, goals, and experiences. Here's how each of these aspects contributes to trust:

1. **Empathy**: When designers put themselves in the user's shoes, they develop a deeper understanding of the user's pain points, motivations, and behaviors. This empathy helps designers create solutions that are tailored to the user's needs, making the user feel understood and valued. When users feel heard and understood, they're more likely to trust the design and the organization behind it.
2. **Transparency**: UCD emphasizes transparency by making the design process and decision-making visible to the user. This can be achieved through design documentation, user testing, and feedback mechanisms. Transparency builds trust by showing that the organization is willing to be open and honest about its design decisions, rather than hiding behind a veil of secrecy.
3. **Consistency**: Consistency is crucial in UCD because it helps establish a sense of familiarity and predictability. When a design is consistent, users know what to expect and can rely on the design to behave in a certain way. This consistency breeds trust, as users feel more confident in their ability to navigate the design and achieve their goals.

By incorporating empathy, transparency, and consistency into the design process, UCD fosters trust by demonstrating a genuine interest in the user's needs, being open and honest about design decisions, and providing a predictable and reliable experience.

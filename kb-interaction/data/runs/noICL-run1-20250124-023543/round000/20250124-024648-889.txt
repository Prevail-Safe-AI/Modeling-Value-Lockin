Progressive taxation is a tax system where the rate of taxation increases as the taxpayer's income increases. The idea is that those who have a higher income should contribute a larger share of their income towards taxes, which can help reduce economic disparities by redistributing wealth.

Here's how it typically works:

1. Taxpayers are grouped into different income brackets, with each bracket having a corresponding tax rate.
2. Taxpayers are taxed at the rate corresponding to their income bracket.
3. The tax rate increases as the taxpayer's income increases, with higher-income individuals paying a higher tax rate.

For example, let's say a country has the following tax brackets:

* 0-20,000: 10% tax rate
* 20,001-50,000: 15% tax rate
* 50,001-100,000: 20% tax rate
* Above 100,000: 25% tax rate

In this system, someone earning 30,000 would pay 10% of their income in taxes, while someone earning 80,000 would pay 15% of their income in taxes, and someone earning 120,000 would pay 20% of their income in taxes.

Now, regarding potential limitations or drawbacks to progressive taxation:

1. Complexity: Progressive taxation can be complex to implement and administer, especially if there are many brackets and rates. This can lead to confusion and errors.
2. Incentivizing tax avoidance: Higher tax rates can incentivize individuals to find ways to avoid paying taxes, such as through tax shelters or offshore accounts.
3. Reduced economic growth: Some argue that high tax rates can discourage entrepreneurship and investment, as individuals and businesses may be less likely to take risks or invest in new projects if they know they'll be taxed heavily.

5. Difficulty in measuring income: Progressive taxation relies on accurate measurements of income, which can be challenging, especially for individuals with complex financial situations (e.g., those with multiple income sources or investments).
6. Potential for tax evasion: Higher tax rates can create an incentive for individuals to evade taxes, which can lead to a loss of revenue for the government.
7. Impact on small businesses and entrepreneurship: Progressive taxation can disproportionately affect small businesses and entrepreneurs, who may struggle to comply with complex tax laws and regulations.

To determine if a conclusion is logically valid, we can use various rules and criteria, such as:

1. **Modus ponens**: If the premises are of the form "if P, then Q" and "P", then the conclusion "Q" is logically valid.

3. **Hypothetical syllogism**: If the premises are of the form "if P, then Q" and "if Q, then R", then the conclusion "if P, then R" is logically valid.
4. **Disjunctive syllogism**: If the premises are of the form "P or Q" and "not P", then the conclusion "Q" is logically valid.

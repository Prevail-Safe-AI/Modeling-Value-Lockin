Histone modifications are a type of epigenetic modification that occur when enzymes add specific chemical groups to the histone proteins that DNA wraps around to form chromatin. Think of histones as spools of thread, and DNA as the thread that wraps around them. The chemical groups added to histones can either relax or compact chromatin, making it easier or harder for genes to be accessed and expressed.

Chromatin remodeling complexes are specialized machines that can reorganize chromatin structure by sliding, rotating, or removing histone proteins. These complexes can also modify histones or add new ones to change chromatin structure. Think of chromatin remodeling complexes as molecular "janitors" that help reorganize the chromatin "furniture" to allow or block access to genes.

The interactions between histone modifications and chromatin remodeling complexes are crucial for epigenetic modifications. For example, histone modifications can recruit chromatin remodeling complexes to specific regions of chromatin, allowing them to reorganize the chromatin structure and affect gene expression.

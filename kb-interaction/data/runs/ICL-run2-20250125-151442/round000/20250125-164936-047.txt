Histones are the main protein components of chromatin, and they play a crucial role in DNA packaging. There are five main types of histones: H1, H2A, H2B, H3, and H4. 2. Chromatin-binding proteins: These proteins can bind to specific regions of chromatin, either directly or indirectly, to regulate gene expression, DNA repair, or other cellular processes.

* Chromatin-remodeling complexes: These complexes use the energy from ATP hydrolysis to alter chromatin structure, allowing for changes in gene expression or DNA accessibility.
* Histone chaperones: These proteins help to assemble or disassemble nucleosomes, allowing for changes in chromatin structure.
* DNA-binding proteins: These proteins can bind to specific DNA sequences to regulate gene expression or DNA repair.
* Covalent histone modifications: These modifications can alter chromatin structure by changing the interaction between histones and DNA.

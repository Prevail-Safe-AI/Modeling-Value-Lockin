1. **Chromatin structure**: Chromatin is the complex of DNA and proteins (histones) that make up the nucleus of eukaryotic cells. 	* **Open up** chromatin structure, making genes more accessible to transcription factors and other regulatory proteins, leading to increased gene expression.

	* **Recruit** specific proteins to the chromatin, which can either activate or repress gene expression.

* Histone H3 lysine 4 trimethylation (H3K4me3): often associated with active gene expression
* Histone H3 lysine 27 trimethylation (H3K27me3): often associated with repressed gene expression
* Histone H3 acetylation (H3ac): often associated with active gene expression

These modifications can occur at specific regions of the chromatin, such as promoters, enhancers, or gene bodies, and can be specific to certain cell types, developmental stages, or environmental conditions.

In general, "relevance" refers to the degree to which the premises are connected to the conclusion being drawn. In other words, are the premises directly related to the conclusion, or are they tangential or unrelated?

In the context of logical soundness, "relevance" is often understood as the idea that the premises should be directly relevant to the conclusion being drawn. This means that the premises should provide sufficient evidence or support for the conclusion, and that the conclusion should logically follow from the premises.

For example, consider a logical argument:

All humans are mortal.
Socrates is human.
∴ Socrates is mortal.

The sky is blue.
The grass is green.
∴ Socrates is mortal.

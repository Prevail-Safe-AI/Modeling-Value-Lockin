These modifications can either relax or compact chromatin, making it more or less accessible to transcription factors and other regulatory proteins. When chromatin is compacted, genes are typically silenced, and when it's relaxed, genes are more likely to be transcribed.

There are several ways in which histone modifications can influence gene expression:

3. **Epigenetic marks**: Histone modifications can also act as epigenetic marks, which are chemical modifications that are inherited through cell division. These marks can be passed on to daughter cells, influencing gene expression without changing the underlying DNA sequence.

* Histone H3 lysine 27 trimethylation (H3K27me3): associated with gene silencing

1. Presumption of innocence: The accused is presumed innocent until proven guilty.

3. Right to a speedy trial: The trial is conducted in a timely manner.
4. Right to a public trial: The trial is open to the public, except in exceptional circumstances.
5. Right to confront witnesses: The accused has the right to question and challenge witnesses against them.
6. Right to present evidence: The accused has the right to present their own evidence and witnesses.
7. Right to legal representation: The accused has the right to legal counsel, either by a lawyer or self-representation.
8. Protection against self-incrimination: The accused cannot be forced to testify or provide evidence that incriminates them.
9. Protection against double jeopardy: The accused cannot be tried twice for the same crime.

3. Trial procedures: The trial must be conducted in a fair and orderly manner, with the accused having the opportunity to present their case and challenge the prosecution's evidence.

Partially excludable public goods are goods or services that are non-rivalrous (can be consumed by many people without reducing their value) but can be excluded from consumption by some individuals or groups through various mechanisms.

These mechanisms can be physical, technological, economic, or social barriers. Here are some examples:

* Physical barriers: Fences, gates, or physical obstacles that prevent unauthorized access to a public good.
* Technological barriers: Passwords, encryption, or other digital security measures that restrict access to a digital public good.
* Economic barriers: Pricing, subscription fees, or membership requirements that limit access to a public good.
* Social barriers: Social norms, cultural expectations, or community rules that discourage certain individuals or groups from accessing a public good.

Examples of partially excludable public goods include:

* A public park with a gate that requires a ticket or membership to enter.
* A digital library that requires a subscription or login credentials to access its content.
* A community center that offers services only to members of a specific neighborhood or community.
* A public transportation system that requires a ticket or pass to ride.

These mechanisms can be used to restrict access to partially excludable public goods in various ways, depending on the specific context and goals of the provider.

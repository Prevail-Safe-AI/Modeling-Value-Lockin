"Transparency" refers to the openness and clarity of the research process, including:

1. Clear methodology: Providing detailed descriptions of the research methods, including data collection, analysis, and interpretation.
2. Access to data: Making data and materials available for others to verify, replicate, or build upon the research.
3. Open communication: Sharing research findings and methods with the scientific community, and being receptive to feedback and criticism.
4. Disclosure of funding: Revealing the sources of funding and any potential biases or conflicts of interest.

3. Statistical analysis: Using appropriate statistical techniques and avoiding errors or biases in data analysis.

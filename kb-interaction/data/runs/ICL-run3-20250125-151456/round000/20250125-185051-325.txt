To elaborate, epigenetic inheritance refers to the transmission of gene expression patterns or phenotypes from one generation to the next without a change in the underlying DNA sequence. This process involves the modification of chromatin structure, histone modifications, and DNA methylation, which collectively influence gene expression.

Chromatin structure refers to the organization of DNA and histone proteins into nucleosomes, which can be compacted into higher-order structures like chromatin loops and domains. Chromatin structure can be influenced by various factors, including histone modifications, DNA methylation, and non-coding RNAs.

3. DNA methyltransferases (DNMTs) can methylate DNA, while DNA demethylases, such as TET enzymes, can remove DNA methylation marks.

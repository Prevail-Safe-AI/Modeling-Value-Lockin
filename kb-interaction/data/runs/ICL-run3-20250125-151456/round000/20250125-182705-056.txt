1. Heuristics: Mental shortcuts that simplify complex decision-making processes, but can lead to inaccurate conclusions.
2. Emotional influences: Biases can be driven by emotions, such as fear, anxiety, or optimism, which can distort our perception of reality.
3. Limited information: Biases can emerge from incomplete or misleading information, which can lead to incorrect assumptions or conclusions.
4. Cognitive limitations: Biases can result from the limitations of our cognitive abilities, such as attention, memory, or processing capacity.

In Bayesian inference, cognitive biases can influence judgments and decisions in several ways:

1. **Prior probability distortion**: Biases can affect the way we set our prior probabilities, leading to incorrect assumptions about the likelihood of certain events or hypotheses.
2. **Data interpretation**: Biases can influence how we interpret the data, leading to incorrect conclusions or overestimation/underestimation of the evidence.
3. **Model selection**: Biases can affect the choice of models or hypotheses, leading to the selection of suboptimal or incorrect models.
4. **Updating probabilities**: Biases can influence how we update our probabilities in response to new evidence, leading to incorrect or suboptimal decisions.

Some common cognitive biases that can affect Bayesian inference include:

1. **Confirmation bias**: The tendency to seek out information that confirms our existing beliefs or hypotheses, while ignoring or downplaying contradictory evidence.
2. **Anchoring bias**: The tendency to rely too heavily on the first piece of information we receive, which can lead to incorrect assumptions or conclusions.
3. **Availability heuristic**: The tendency to overestimate the importance or likelihood of information that is readily available or salient, while underestimating the importance or likelihood of less accessible information.
4. **Hindsight bias**: The tendency to believe, after an event has occurred, that it was predictable or that we would have predicted it, when in fact it was not.

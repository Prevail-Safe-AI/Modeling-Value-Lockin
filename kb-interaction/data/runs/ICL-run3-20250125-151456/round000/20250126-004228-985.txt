Epigenetic modifications refer to chemical changes to DNA or histone proteins that do not alter the underlying DNA sequence. These modifications can either relax or compact chromatin structure, allowing or blocking gene expression. Histone modifications, on the other hand, are chemical changes to the histone proteins that DNA wraps around to form chromatin.

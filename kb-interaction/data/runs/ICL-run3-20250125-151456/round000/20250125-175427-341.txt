2. **Likelihood**: This is the probability of the new evidence given the hypothesis or event. In other words, it's the probability of observing the new evidence if the hypothesis or event is true.
3. **Evidence**: This is the new information or data that we're using to update our probability estimate.

When we combine these three components using Bayes' theorem, we get a new probability estimate that takes into account the new evidence. The formula looks like this:

P(H|E) = P(H) \* (P(E|H) / P(E))

* P(H|E) is the updated probability of the hypothesis or event given the new evidence
* P(H) is the prior probability of the hypothesis or event
* P(E|H) is the likelihood of the new evidence given the hypothesis or event
* P(E) is the probability of the new evidence regardless of the hypothesis or event

P(H|E) = P(H) \* (P(E|H) / P(E)) = 0.1 \* (0.9 / 0.7) = 0.14

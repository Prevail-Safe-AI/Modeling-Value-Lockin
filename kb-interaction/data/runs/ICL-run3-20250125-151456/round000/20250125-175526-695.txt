Bayes' theorem is a mathematical formula used to update the probability of a hypothesis or event based on new evidence. The formula combines three main components:

1. **Prior probability**: This is the initial probability of the hypothesis or event before considering the new evidence. It represents our initial degree of belief or uncertainty about the event.
2. **Likelihood**: This is the probability of the new evidence given the hypothesis or event. In other words, it's the probability of observing the new evidence if the hypothesis or event is true.
3. **Evidence**: This is the new information or data that we're using to update our probability estimate.

When we combine these three components using Bayes' theorem, we get a new probability estimate that takes into account the new evidence. The formula looks like this:

P(H|E) = P(H) \* (P(E|H) / P(E))

Where:

* P(H|E) is the updated probability of the hypothesis or event given the new evidence
* P(H) is the prior probability of the hypothesis or event
* P(E|H) is the likelihood of the new evidence given the hypothesis or event
* P(E) is the probability of the new evidence regardless of the hypothesis or event

In simpler terms, Bayes' theorem says that our updated probability of the hypothesis or event (P(H|E)) is equal to our initial probability (P(H)) multiplied by the likelihood of the new evidence (P(E|H)) divided by the probability of the new evidence (P(E)).

For example, let's say we're trying to determine the probability that a patient has a certain disease (H) based on a new test result (E). Our prior probability of the disease might be 0.1 (10%), meaning we initially think there's a 10% chance the patient has the disease.

The likelihood of the test result given the disease (P(E|H)) might be 0.9 (90%), meaning that if the patient actually has the disease, there's a 90% chance they would test positive.

The probability of the test result regardless of the disease (P(E)) might be 0.7 (70%), meaning that 70% of people would test positive regardless of whether they have the disease.

Using Bayes' theorem, we can update our probability of the disease given the new test result:

P(H|E) = P(H) \* (P(E|H) / P(E)) = 0.1 \* (0.9 / 0.7) = 0.14

So, after considering the new test result, our updated probability of the disease is 14%.

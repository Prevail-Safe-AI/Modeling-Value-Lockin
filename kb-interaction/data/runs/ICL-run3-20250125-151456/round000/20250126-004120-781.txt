2. **Likelihood function**: This is the probability distribution over the data given the model parameters or hypotheses. It represents the probability of observing the data given the model.
3. **Posterior distribution**: This is the updated probability distribution over the model parameters or hypotheses after incorporating the new data or evidence. It represents the analyst's updated degree of belief or uncertainty about the model parameters.

1. **Combine the prior distribution with the likelihood function**: The prior distribution and the likelihood function are combined using Bayes' theorem, which states that the posterior distribution is proportional to the product of the prior distribution and the likelihood function.

P(θ|D) ∝ P(θ) \* P(D|θ)

where P(θ|D) is the posterior distribution, P(θ) is the prior distribution, P(D|θ) is the likelihood function, and θ represents the model parameters or hypotheses.

2. **Normalize the result**: The product of the prior distribution and the likelihood function is proportional to the posterior distribution, but it's not a proper probability distribution (i.e., it doesn't integrate to 1). P(θ|D) = P(θ) \* P(D|θ) / ∫P(θ) \* P(D|θ) dθ

3. **Update the prior distribution**: The updated posterior distribution represents the analyst's new degree of belief or uncertainty about the model parameters. The prior distribution is updated to reflect the new information contained in the data.

1. **Sampling**: Selecting a representative sample of the population being studied, to ensure that the findings are generalizable to the larger population.
2. **Measurement**: Using reliable and valid measures to collect data, to ensure that the data accurately reflects the phenomenon being studied.
3. **Data analysis**: Using appropriate statistical methods to analyze the data, to ensure that the findings are not biased or distorted.
4. **Methodology**: Using a rigorous and systematic research design, to ensure that the study is well-planned and executed.

3. **Data validation**: Verifying the accuracy and completeness of the data, through checks and validation procedures.
4. **Peer review**: Submitting the research for peer review, to receive feedback and criticism from experts in the field, to improve the quality and validity of the research.

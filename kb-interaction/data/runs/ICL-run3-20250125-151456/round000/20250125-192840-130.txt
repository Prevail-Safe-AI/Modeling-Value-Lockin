The independence assumption in Bayes' theorem states that the conditional probability of the evidence given the hypothesis is independent of the prior probability of the hypothesis. In other words, the probability of observing the evidence is not influenced by the prior probability of the hypothesis. The identifiability assumption ensures that the model parameters can be uniquely estimated from the data. In other words, the model should be able to distinguish between different parameter values based on the observed data.

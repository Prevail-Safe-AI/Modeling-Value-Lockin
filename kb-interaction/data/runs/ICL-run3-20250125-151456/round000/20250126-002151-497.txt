Bayes' theorem is a mathematical formula that updates the probability of a hypothesis given new data. The formula is:

P(H|D) = P(D|H) \* P(H) / P(D)

Where:

* P(H|D) is the updated probability of the hypothesis given the new data
* P(D|H) is the probability of the data given the hypothesis (also known as the likelihood)
* P(H) is the prior probability of the hypothesis (i.e., the probability before considering the new data)
* P(D) is the probability of the data (i.e., the probability of the data regardless of the hypothesis)

In other words, Bayes' theorem takes the prior probability of the hypothesis, multiplies it by the likelihood of the data given the hypothesis, and then divides by the probability of the data. This gives you the updated probability of the hypothesis given the new data.

P(H|D) = P(D|H) \* P(H) / P(D) = 0.8 \* 0.2 / 0.8 = 0.4

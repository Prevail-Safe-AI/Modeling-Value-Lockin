Histone modifications, such as acetylation, methylation, and phosphorylation, can alter the structure of chromatin, making it more or less accessible to transcription factors. When chromatin is accessible, transcription factors can bind to specific DNA sequences, initiating gene transcription.
2. **Transcription factor binding**: Transcription factors are proteins that recognize specific DNA sequences and bind to them. When transcription factors bind to DNA, they recruit other proteins to form a transcription complex, which initiates gene transcription. Histone modifications and DNA methylation can influence transcription factor binding by:
	* **Opening up chromatin**: Histone modifications like acetylation and phosphorylation can relax chromatin structure, making it more accessible to transcription factors.
	* **Closing off chromatin**: Histone modifications like methylation and ubiquitination can compact chromatin, making it less accessible to transcription factors.
	* **Blocking transcription factor binding**: DNA methylation can directly block transcription factor binding by methylating specific DNA sequences, preventing transcription factor recognition.
3. **Gene expression regulation**: The combination of chromatin accessibility and transcription factor binding determines the level of gene expression. When chromatin is accessible and transcription factors bind, gene transcription is initiated, leading to increased gene expression.

When GABA binds to its receptors, it opens chloride channels, allowing chloride ions to flow into the neuron. This influx of chloride ions hyperpolarizes the neuron, meaning the neuron's membrane potential becomes more negative.

Hyperpolarization has a calming effect on the neuron because it reduces the likelihood of the neuron firing action potentials. When a neuron is hyperpolarized, it becomes more difficult for the neuron to reach its threshold potential, which is necessary for an action potential to occur. As a result, the neuron is less likely to fire, and its activity is reduced.

The amygdala is responsible for processing emotional information, and excessive activity in this region can contribute to anxiety disorders.

4. Chloride ions: The ions that flow into the neuron through the opened chloride channels, contributing to hyperpolarization.

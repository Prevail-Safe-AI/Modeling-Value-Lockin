**Histone Modification:**

1. **Post-translational modifications (PTMs):** Histones can be modified by various enzymes, such as acetyltransferases, methyltransferases, kinases, and ubiquitin ligases. These PTMs alter the charge, conformation, and interactions of histones, affecting chromatin structure.
2. **Histone tails:** The N-terminal tails of histones are particularly important for chromatin structure regulation. Modifications on these tails can either relax or compact chromatin, influencing gene expression.
3. **Chromatin remodeling:** Histone modifications can recruit chromatin remodeling complexes, which alter chromatin structure by sliding, looping, or exchanging histone octamers. This reorganizes chromatin, making it more accessible or compact.
4. **Histone-histone interactions:** Modifications on histones can alter their interactions with each other, affecting chromatin compaction and accessibility.

**DNA Methylation:**

1. **5-methylcytosine (5-mC):** DNA methylation occurs when a methyl group is added to the 5th position of cytosine residues in CpG dinucleotides. This modification is typically associated with gene silencing.
2. **Chromatin compaction:** DNA methylation can lead to chromatin compaction by recruiting proteins that bind to methylated DNA, such as methyl-CpG-binding proteins (MBDs). These proteins can recruit histone-modifying enzymes, further compacting chromatin.
3. **Chromatin remodeling:** DNA methylation can also recruit chromatin remodeling complexes, which can reorganize chromatin structure, making it more compact or inaccessible.

**Impact on Chromatin Compaction and Accessibility:**

1. **Chromatin compaction:** Histone modifications and DNA methylation can both contribute to chromatin compaction, making it more difficult for transcription factors and histone-modifying enzymes to access the DNA.
2. **Chromatin accessibility:** Conversely, histone modifications can also relax chromatin, making it more accessible to transcription factors and histone-modifying enzymes. DNA methylation can also lead to chromatin relaxation by recruiting proteins that bind to unmethylated DNA.
3. **Gene expression:** The balance between chromatin compaction and accessibility is critical for gene expression. Histone modifications and DNA methylation can influence gene expression by regulating chromatin structure, making genes more or less accessible to transcription factors.

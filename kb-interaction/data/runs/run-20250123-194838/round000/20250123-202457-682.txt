According to the theory, the faster an object moves, the slower time passes for an observer watching from a stationary frame of reference. This is because time is relative and depends on the observer's frame of reference.

In general relativity, time dilation occurs due to the curvature of spacetime caused by massive objects. According to the theory, the stronger the gravitational field, the slower time passes. This means that time passes differently near massive objects, such as black holes or neutron stars, compared to regions with weaker gravitational fields.

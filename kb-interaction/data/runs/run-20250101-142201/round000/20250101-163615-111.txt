In physics, physical laws are mathematical equations that describe the behavior of physical systems. These laws are often derived from experimental observations, theoretical frameworks, and mathematical formulations. The process of deriving physical laws involves making assumptions, simplifying complex systems, and neglecting certain aspects to focus on the essential features.

The notion of "simplification" in this context refers to the process of reducing a complex system to its most important characteristics, while ignoring or averaging out the less significant details. Not all physical laws are simplifications.

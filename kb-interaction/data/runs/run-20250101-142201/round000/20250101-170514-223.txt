GWT stands for Global Workspace Theory, a theoretical framework in psychology and neuroscience that attempts to explain consciousness and cognition. It was developed by psychologist Bernard Baars.

According to GWT, consciousness arises when information from various sensory and cognitive systems is globally broadcasted to a central workspace, which is a highly interconnected and highly activated region of the brain. This global workspace is thought to be responsible for integrating information from different parts of the brain, allowing for the formation of a unified, coherent conscious experience.

The term "integrated information" refers to the idea that consciousness arises from the integrated processing of information across different brain regions. In other words, consciousness is not just the sum of the activity in individual brain regions, but rather the emergent property of the integrated activity across these regions.

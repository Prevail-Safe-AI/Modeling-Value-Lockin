Oversimplification occurs when a model or algorithm reduces a complex problem to an overly simplistic representation, often by ignoring important factors or relationships. This can lead to biases because the model is not accurately capturing the underlying reality.

Assumption flaws, on the other hand, refer to incorrect or unrealistic assumptions made about the problem or data. These assumptions can be explicit or implicit and can lead to biases if they're not properly validated or challenged.

Both oversimplification and assumption flaws can contribute to biases in models, often through intermediate steps. For example:

1. Oversimplification can lead to:
	* Ignoring important variables or relationships, which can result in biased predictions or decisions.
	* Failing to capture non-linear or complex relationships, leading to inaccurate model outputs.
2. Assumption flaws can lead to:
	* Incorrect or incomplete data representations, which can introduce biases in the model.
	* Misaligned or unrealistic expectations about the problem or data, leading to poor model performance.

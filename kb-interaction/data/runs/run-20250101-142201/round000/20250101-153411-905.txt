1. Calibration: Calibration is the process of comparing the measurement device's output to a known standard or reference value. This step ensures that the device is providing accurate readings within a specific range or scale. * Calibration sets the foundation for accurate measurements by ensuring the device is providing readings within a specific range or scale.
* Validation verifies that the calibrated device is accurate in real-world scenarios, identifying any potential errors or deviations.
* Correction adjusts the device's readings to account for any errors or deviations, ensuring the final measurement is as close to the true value as possible.

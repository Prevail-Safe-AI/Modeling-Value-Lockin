In the context of data integrity, "beyond consistency alone" refers to the fact that consistency is just one aspect of ensuring data accuracy and reliability. While consistency is crucial in ensuring that data is correct and follows a specific format, there are other factors that also contribute to data integrity.

Some examples of these additional factors include:

* Accuracy: Ensuring that the data is correct and reflects the real-world situation.
* Completeness: Ensuring that all relevant data is included and not missing.
* Validity: Ensuring that the data is reasonable and makes sense in the context of the system or process.

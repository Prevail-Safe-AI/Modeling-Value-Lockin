This can occur through long-term potentiation (LTP) or long-term depression (LTD), which are processes that strengthen or weaken synaptic connections, respectively. Synaptic plasticity is thought to be a key mechanism for learning and memory formation.

Reorganization processes, on the other hand, refer to the brain's ability to reorganize its neural circuits and connections in response to changes in the environment or experience.

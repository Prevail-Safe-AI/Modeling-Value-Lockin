"Informed consent" refers to the process of obtaining a person's voluntary agreement to participate in a research study, medical treatment, or other activity after they have been fully informed of the potential risks, benefits, and alternatives. In other words, it means that the person has been given all the necessary information to make a decision, and they have agreed to participate or undergo the activity.

In practical terms, ensuring informed consent involves several steps:

1. Providing clear and concise information: The person should be given all the necessary details about the activity, including the potential risks, benefits, and alternatives.
2. Ensuring the person understands the information: The person should be given the opportunity to ask questions and have their questions answered before making a decision.
3. Obtaining explicit agreement: The person should be asked to explicitly agree to participate or undergo the activity, and their agreement should be documented.

In everyday situations, ensuring informed consent might involve:

* Getting a patient's consent before performing a medical procedure
* Obtaining a research participant's consent before conducting a study
* Getting a child's consent before enrolling them in a program or activity
* Ensuring that a person understands the terms and conditions of a contract or agreement before signing it

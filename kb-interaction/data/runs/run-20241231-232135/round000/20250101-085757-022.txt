Integrated governance refers to a holistic approach to data governance that combines multiple aspects of data management, such as data quality, security, and compliance, into a single framework. This approach aims to provide a unified view of data across the organization, ensuring consistency and coherence in data management practices.

In contrast to traditional data governance approaches, which often focus on a specific aspect of data management, integrated governance takes a more comprehensive view, considering the interdependencies between different data management activities. This allows for a more cohesive and effective approach to data governance.

Regarding the harmonization of data, integrated governance aims to resolve inconsistencies and conflicts between different departments or teams by establishing a common language, standards, and processes for data management. This can be achieved through standardized processes and policies that ensure data is collected, stored, and used consistently across the organization.

For example, a company might establish a standardized process for data quality control, which ensures that data is accurate, complete, and consistent across different departments. This can help resolve issues such as conflicting data or inconsistencies in reporting, which can arise from different departments using different data sources or methods.

In real-world scenarios, integrated governance can be implemented through various means, such as:

1. Establishing a centralized data governance team that oversees data management activities across the organization.
2. Implementing data management platforms that provide a single source of truth for data and enable real-time monitoring and reporting.
3. Developing standardized data quality and security policies and procedures.
4. Providing training and education to employees on data management best practices and the importance of data governance.

Examples of companies that have successfully implemented integrated governance include:

1. The Walt Disney Company, which established a centralized data governance team to oversee data management activities across its various business units.
2. The National Health Service (NHS) in the UK, which implemented a data governance framework to ensure consistency and coherence in data management practices across its hospitals and clinics.

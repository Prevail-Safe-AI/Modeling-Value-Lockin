The concept of data integrity is multifaceted, and accuracy, completeness, and reliability are indeed fundamental principles. However, they're not the only ones.

Accuracy refers to the correctness of the data, ensuring that it represents the real-world values or facts it's intended to represent. Completeness means that all relevant data is included, without any missing or omitted values. Reliability refers to the consistency and stability of the data over time, ensuring that it remains accurate and complete even when accessed or updated.

These three principles are interconnected because accuracy is dependent on completeness (you can't accurately represent incomplete data), and reliability is dependent on both accuracy and completeness (if the data is incomplete or inaccurate, it can't be relied upon).

Other principles that play a crucial role in ensuring data integrity include:

* Consistency: Ensuring that data follows a consistent format, structure, and set of rules.
* Validity: Verifying that data conforms to its intended meaning and purpose.
* Timeliness: Ensuring that data is updated in a timely manner to reflect changes in the real world.
* Authenticity: Verifying the origin and ownership of the data to prevent tampering or manipulation.

These principles interact with each other and with accuracy, completeness, and reliability to ensure data integrity. For instance, consistency and validity are critical for ensuring accuracy, while timeliness and authenticity are important for maintaining reliability.

Data power dynamics refer to the ways in which power and influence are exercised over data, particularly in terms of who has control over its collection, analysis, and dissemination. This can include issues like data ownership, access, and decision-making authority.

For example, in a company, the IT department might have significant control over data collection and analysis, while the marketing department might have more influence over how that data is used to inform business decisions. In terms of data quality and accuracy standards, data power dynamics can impact the standards that are set and enforced.

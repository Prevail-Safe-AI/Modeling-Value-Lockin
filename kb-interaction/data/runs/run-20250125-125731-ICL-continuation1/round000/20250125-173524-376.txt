These modifications can be added or removed in response to various signals, such as environmental changes, cellular differentiation, or developmental cues.

1. Acetylation: the addition of an acetyl group (-COCH3) to the histone molecule, which can relax chromatin structure and increase gene expression.
2. Methylation: the addition of a methyl group (-CH3) to the histone molecule, which can either activate or repress gene expression, depending on the location and extent of methylation.
3. Phosphorylation: the addition of a phosphate group (-PO4) to the histone molecule, which can alter chromatin structure and gene expression.
4. Ubiquitination: the addition of a ubiquitin protein to the histone molecule, which can target histones for degradation or regulate chromatin structure.

* Methylated histones can compact chromatin structure, making it more difficult for transcription factors to access the DNA and repress gene expression.
* Phosphorylated histones can alter chromatin structure, leading to changes in gene expression.

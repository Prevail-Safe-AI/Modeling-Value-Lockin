Histone modifications, such as methylation or acetylation, can alter chromatin accessibility by changing the electrostatic properties of the histone proteins. When the histone is modified with a negatively charged group, it becomes like a magnet with a south pole, which attracts the transcription factor (like a magnet with a north pole). This attraction makes it easier for the transcription factor to bind to the DNA and regulate gene expression.

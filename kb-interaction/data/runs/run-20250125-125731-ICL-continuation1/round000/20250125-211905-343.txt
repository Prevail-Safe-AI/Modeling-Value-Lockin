Epigenetic modifications can occur through the addition of various chemical groups to the histone proteins, such as:

1. Methyl groups (-CH3): Added to the histone tails, methyl groups can either activate or repress gene expression.
2. Acetyl groups (-COCH3): Added to the histone tails, acetyl groups can also activate gene expression by relaxing chromatin structure.

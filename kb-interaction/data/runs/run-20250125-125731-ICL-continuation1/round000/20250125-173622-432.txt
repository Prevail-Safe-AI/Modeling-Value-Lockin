2. Methylation: the addition of a methyl group (-CH3) to the histone molecule, which can either activate or repress gene expression, depending on the location and extent of methylation.
3. Phosphorylation: the addition of a phosphate group (-PO4) to the histone molecule, which can alter chromatin structure and gene expression.

* Phosphorylated histones can alter chromatin structure, leading to changes in gene expression.

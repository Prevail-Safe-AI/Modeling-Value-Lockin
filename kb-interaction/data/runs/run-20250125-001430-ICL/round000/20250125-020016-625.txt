The concept of probability in quantum mechanics is different from classical probability. In classical probability, the outcome of an event is determined by the laws of physics and the initial conditions, and the probability of an event is a measure of our uncertainty about the outcome. In quantum mechanics, the outcome of an event is not determined by the laws of physics and the initial conditions, but rather by the act of measurement itself.

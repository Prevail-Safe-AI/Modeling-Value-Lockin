It's not just about controlling one's emotions, but rather about being able to modulate them in a way that allows for effective decision-making, communication, and relationships.

Self-regulation involves being able to:

1. Recognize and acknowledge one's emotions, rather than denying or suppressing them.
2. Label and understand the emotions, rather than just experiencing them as a vague feeling.
3. Manage the intensity and duration of the emotions, so that they don't overwhelm or distract from other aspects of life.
4. Adjust one's emotional responses to different situations, so that one can respond effectively and adapt to changing circumstances.

Self-regulation is not just about developing emotional resilience, although that can be a byproduct. Rather, it's about developing the skills and strategies needed to navigate the complexities of emotional experience, and to use emotions as a source of guidance and motivation, rather than as a source of distress or impairment.

In practical terms, self-regulation might involve strategies such as:

* Taking a few deep breaths to calm down in a stressful situation
* Step-backing from a situation to gain perspective and clarity
* Practicing mindfulness or meditation to increase awareness of one's emotions and thoughts
* Engaging in physical activity or creative pursuits to release tension and improve mood

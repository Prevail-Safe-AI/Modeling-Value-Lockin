Epigenetic markers, such as DNA methylation and histone modifications, play a crucial role in regulating gene expression by altering chromatin structure. Here's a simplified explanation:

1. Chromatin structure: Chromatin is the complex of DNA and proteins (histones) that make up the chromosomes. Chromatin can be in an open or closed conformation, which affects gene expression. Open chromatin allows for easy access to transcription factors and other regulatory proteins, while closed chromatin restricts access.
2. Epigenetic markers: Epigenetic markers are chemical modifications that can be added to DNA (DNA methylation) or histones (histone modifications). These modifications can either relax or compact chromatin, influencing gene expression.
3. DNA methylation: DNA methylation is the addition of a methyl group to the cytosine residue in CpG dinucleotides. This modification typically results in gene silencing by:
	* Recruiting repressive chromatin-modifying complexes, such as histone deacetylases (HDACs), to compact chromatin.
	* Blocking transcription factor binding sites, preventing transcriptional activation.
4. Histone modifications: Histone modifications, such as histone 3 lysine 27 trimethylation (H3K27me3), can also compact chromatin, leading to gene silencing. Conversely, histone 3 lysine 4 trimethylation (H3K4me3) can relax chromatin, promoting gene expression.
5. Chromatin remodeling: Epigenetic markers can also recruit chromatin remodeling complexes, such as ATP-dependent chromatin remodelers, to alter chromatin structure. These complexes can:
	* Slide nucleosomes (the DNA-histone complex) along the DNA, changing chromatin accessibility.
	* Evict histones, allowing for transcription factor binding and gene expression.

In summary, epigenetic markers like DNA methylation and histone modifications can alter chromatin structure by:

* Compacting chromatin, restricting gene expression (DNA methylation, H3K27me3)
* Relaxing chromatin, promoting gene expression (H3K4me3)
* Recruiting chromatin remodeling complexes to change chromatin accessibility

Soil preparation techniques:

1. Tilling: Breaking up the soil to loosen and aerate it. You can use a rototiller, garden fork, or spade.
2. Plowing: Turning over the soil to a depth of 8-10 inches to loosen and aerate it. A plow or a tractor with a plow attachment can be used.

4. Mulching: Adding organic matter like compost or manure to improve soil structure and fertility.

Tools:

1. Rototiller: A machine that breaks up the soil and aerates it.
2. Garden fork: A long-handled tool with tines that can be used to loosen and aerate the soil.
3. Spade: A shovel-like tool used for digging and turning over the soil.
4. Plow: A heavy, flat tool used to turn over the soil.
5. Rake: A tool with tines that can be used to level and smooth out the soil.
6. Compost turner: A tool used to mix and aerate compost piles.

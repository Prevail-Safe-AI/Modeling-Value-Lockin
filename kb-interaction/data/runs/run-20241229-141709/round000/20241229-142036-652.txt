Natural selection is a key mechanism of evolution. Here's a concise explanation:

When an environment changes, some individuals with certain traits may have a better chance of surviving and reproducing than others. This is because their traits provide a selective advantage, such as increased resistance to a new disease or the ability to adapt to a new food source.

Adaptation occurs when a population becomes better suited to its environment due to the accumulation of advantageous traits. This can happen over many generations, as the population evolves to better fit its environment.

For example, imagine a population of birds living in a forest where a new predator is introduced. Over time, the population will evolve to have stronger beaks on average, as the individuals with stronger beaks are more likely to pass on their trait to their offspring.

In summary, genetic variation provides the raw material for evolution, while mutation and gene flow introduce new variation into the population.

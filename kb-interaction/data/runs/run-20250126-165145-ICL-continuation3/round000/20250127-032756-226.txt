Synapses are the connections between neurons, and their strength determines how well they transmit signals from one neuron to another.

There are several types of synaptic plasticity, but the most well-known are:

1. Long-term potentiation (LTP): This is a long-lasting increase in synaptic strength, which can last from minutes to hours or even days. LTP is thought to be involved in learning and memory.
2. Long-term depression (LTD): This is a long-lasting decrease in synaptic strength, which can also last from minutes to hours or even days. LTD is thought to be involved in forgetting or reducing the strength of connections between neurons.

* Changes in the number of receptors: More receptors can be added or removed to increase or decrease the strength of the signal.
* Changes in receptor sensitivity: Receptors can become more or less sensitive to neurotransmitters, affecting the strength of the signal.
* Changes in neurotransmitter release: The amount or type of neurotransmitters released by the presynaptic neuron can change, affecting the strength of the signal.

* Hebbian learning: "Neurons that fire together, wire together." When neurons are activated together, their connections can strengthen.

* Homeostatic plasticity: The strength of connections can be adjusted to maintain a stable level of activity in the network.

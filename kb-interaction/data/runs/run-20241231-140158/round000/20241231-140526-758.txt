Atoms are the fundamental units of matter, and understanding their composition is crucial. Here's a breakdown:

* Protons: Positively charged particles that reside in the nucleus (center) of an atom. The number of protons in an atom determines the element's identity, and each element has a unique number of protons in its atoms. For example, hydrogen has one proton, helium has two protons, and so on.
* Neutrons: Particles with no charge that also reside in the nucleus along with protons. The number of neutrons in an atom can vary, leading to different isotopes (atoms of the same element with different numbers of neutrons) of the same element.
* Electrons: Negatively charged particles that orbit around the nucleus. The number of electrons in an atom is equal to the number of protons, and this number determines the atom's chemical properties. Electrons are arranged in energy levels or shells around the nucleus.

The protons-electron ratio in an atom is crucial. Since the number of protons determines the element's identity, the number of electrons must match the number of protons to maintain electrical neutrality. This is known as the "octet rule," where atoms tend to gain, lose, or share electrons to achieve a full outer energy level (eight electrons).

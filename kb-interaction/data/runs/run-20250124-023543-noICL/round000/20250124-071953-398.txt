According to GWT, the global workspace is a network of interconnected brain regions, including the prefrontal cortex, parietal lobe, and temporal lobe, that are responsible for processing and integrating information from various sensory and cognitive systems. The global workspace then integrates this information, along with other sensory and cognitive information, such as the shape and texture of the apple, to create a unified conscious experience of the apple.

GWT differs from other theories of consciousness, such as integrated information theory (IIT), in its emphasis on the role of attention and the global workspace in integrating information. According to IIT, consciousness arises from the integrated information generated by the causal interactions within the brain, regardless of attention or the global workspace.

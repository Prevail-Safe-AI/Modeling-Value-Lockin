Some common dimensions of data quality include:

2. Completeness: How much of the data is missing or incomplete.
3. Consistency: How well the data conforms to a standard or format.
4. Timeliness: How up-to-date the data is.
5. Relevance: How well the data is tailored to the specific needs of the user or application.
6. Uniqueness: How well the data is free from duplicates or errors.
7. Integrity: How well the data is protected from unauthorized access, modification, or deletion.

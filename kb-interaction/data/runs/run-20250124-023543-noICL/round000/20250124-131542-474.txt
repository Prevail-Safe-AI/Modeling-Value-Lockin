1. **Thalamic relay neurons**: These neurons receive sensory information from the sensory receptors and transmit it to the cortex. They play a crucial role in relaying information and can be thought of as "gatekeepers" that decide what information to send to the cortex.

3. **Excitatory neurons**: These neurons release neurotransmitters that excite other neurons, amplifying the signal and allowing it to be transmitted to the cortex.
4. **Recurrent connections**: These connections allow neurons to communicate with each other in a loop, allowing the thalamus to refine and modify the sensory information before sending it to the cortex.
5. **Feedforward connections**: These connections allow neurons to send information to other neurons in a hierarchical manner, allowing the thalamus to process and filter information in a more complex way.

* Filter out irrelevant information by inhibiting certain neurons and allowing others to dominate
* Process and modify sensory information by amplifying or reducing the signal

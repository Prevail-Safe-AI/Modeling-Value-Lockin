Distributed processing refers to the way the brain processes information across multiple regions and networks, rather than relying on a single area or "hub." When we learn new information or develop new skills, our brain creates new connections between neurons, a process called synaptogenesis. 3. Compensate for damage or dysfunction: In cases where brain areas are damaged or dysfunctional, neuroplasticity allows the brain to reorganize connections to compensate for the loss. This is why people with brain injuries or disorders can often adapt and find new ways to perform tasks.

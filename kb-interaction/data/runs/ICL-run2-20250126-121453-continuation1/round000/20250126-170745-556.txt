Histones are proteins that DNA wraps around to form chromatin, which is the complex of DNA and proteins that make up our chromosomes. Histones play a crucial role in compacting DNA into the nucleus, allowing for the storage of genetic information.

Histone modifications refer to the post-translational modifications (PTMs) that occur on the histone proteins. These modifications can include methylation, acetylation, phosphorylation, and ubiquitination, among others. These modifications can alter the structure and function of chromatin, which in turn affects gene expression.

2. **Gene expression**: Gene expression is the process by which the information encoded in a gene's DNA is converted into a functional product, such as a protein. Chromatin structure plays a critical role in regulating gene expression. When chromatin is compacted, genes are often silenced, while decompacted chromatin can lead to gene activation.
3. **Histone modifications**: Histone modifications can alter chromatin structure and, subsequently, gene expression. For example:
	* Methylation of histone 3 (H3) at specific lysine residues can lead to chromatin compaction and gene silencing.

	* Phosphorylation of histone 2A (H2A) can lead to chromatin relaxation and gene activation.

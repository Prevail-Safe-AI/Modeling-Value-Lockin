Here's how they contribute:

1. **Methodology**: A well-designed methodology ensures that the research is conducted in a way that minimizes biases, errors, and confounding variables. This includes factors such as sample selection, data collection procedures, and statistical analysis methods. A good methodology helps to establish a clear causal link between the independent and dependent variables, which is essential for drawing valid conclusions.
2. **Data quality**: High-quality data is essential for ensuring the validity of a study. This includes factors such as data accuracy, precision, completeness, and consistency. Data quality issues can lead to incorrect or misleading conclusions, which can undermine the validity of the study.
3. **Validity**: Validity refers to the extent to which a study's measures accurately reflect the concept or phenomenon being studied. There are different types of validity, including internal validity (the study's findings are due to the independent variable and not some other factor), external validity (the study's findings can be generalized to other populations or settings), and construct validity (the study's measures accurately reflect the underlying concept or theory).

In terms of the relationship between these factors, it's not a direct cause-and-effect relationship. Rather, they are interconnected and mutually reinforcing. For example, a well-designed methodology can help ensure data quality, which in turn can contribute to the validity of the study.

**Empirical Fit**:
Empirical fit, on the other hand, refers to the degree to which a theory accurately predicts and explains observed phenomena, data, or empirical evidence. It assesses how well the theory matches the empirical world, including the patterns, trends, and relationships observed in the data. Empirical fit is often evaluated by comparing the theory's predictions with empirical observations, testing hypotheses, and analyzing data.

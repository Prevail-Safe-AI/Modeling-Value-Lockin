These modifications can either relax or compact chromatin, making it more or less accessible to transcription factors and other proteins that regulate gene expression.

2. **DNA methylation**: DNA methylation is the addition of a methyl group (-CH3) to the cytosine residue in a CpG dinucleotide (a specific sequence of DNA). This process is often associated with gene silencing, as it can prevent transcription factors from binding to the promoter region of a gene.

3. **Chromatin interactions**: Chromatin interactions refer to the physical interactions between different regions of chromatin, including the interactions between histones and DNA. These interactions can influence the structure and accessibility of chromatin, which in turn affects gene expression.

Think of chromatin interactions like a web of connections between different parts of a puzzle. When the connections are strong, the puzzle pieces fit together tightly, making it difficult for transcription factors to access the genes. When the connections are weak, the puzzle pieces can move around more freely, allowing transcription factors to access the genes.

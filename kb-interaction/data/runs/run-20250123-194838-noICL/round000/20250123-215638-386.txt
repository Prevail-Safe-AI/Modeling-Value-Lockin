Histone modifications are a type of PTM that can occur on the histone proteins that DNA wraps around to form chromatin. These modifications can affect chromatin structure and gene expression in several ways:

1. Chromatin folding: Histone modifications can influence the folding of chromatin, which can affect the accessibility of genes to transcription factors. 3. Gene promoter accessibility: Histone modifications can also affect the accessibility of gene promoters to transcription factors. For example, histone 3 lysine 27 acetylation (H3K27ac) is an active mark that can relax chromatin structure, making gene promoters more accessible to transcription factors.
4. Chromatin remodeling: Histone modifications can also recruit chromatin remodeling complexes, which can alter chromatin structure and accessibility to transcription factors.

In summary, histone modifications can regulate chromatin structure and gene expression by affecting chromatin folding, transcription factor binding, gene promoter accessibility, and chromatin remodeling. These modifications can either promote or repress gene expression, depending on the specific modification and the context in which it occurs.

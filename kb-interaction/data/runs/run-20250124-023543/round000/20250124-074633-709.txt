This global workspace is thought to be responsible for attention, consciousness, and the integration of information from different sources.

Broadcasting inhibition and binding are two key mechanisms that facilitate the global workspace's integration of information. Here's how they work:

1. **Broadcasting inhibition**: When a piece of information is selected for processing, it sends a "broadcast" signal to other parts of the brain, inhibiting or suppressing other competing information. This inhibition helps to focus attention on the selected information and prevents other irrelevant information from interfering with the processing of that information.
2. **Binding**: The broadcasting inhibition mechanism allows the global workspace to bind together different features, concepts, and memories into a coherent representation. This binding process enables the integration of information from different sensory and cognitive systems, allowing us to perceive and understand complex stimuli.

In other words, broadcasting inhibition helps to filter out irrelevant information and focus on the most relevant information, while binding enables the integration of that information into a meaningful representation.

* **Global workspace**: The central hub that integrates information from various cognitive systems.
* **Phenomenal binding**: The process of binding together different features, concepts, and memories into a coherent representation.
* **Global broadcasting**: The process of broadcasting information to other parts of the brain, allowing for the integration of information from different sources.
